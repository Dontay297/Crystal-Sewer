Godot engine game.

A Silly first-Person Horror game where an experimental Stickman from a research facility has to capture samples of a crystalline creature that has escaped. 

Goal: The Stickman has to gather 8 orbs in order to open a door and escape.

Obstacles/ Enemies: Crystalline Creature

A creature that escaped from a research facility and ran into the sewer. This creature can only go into a straight line from each corridor until you reach 4 orbs then it evolves to a bipedal creature that can stalks the sewers. 


Orb and Creature Mechanics
1. Orb Appearance & Placement
Visuals: Glowing, crystalline spheres, hovering slightly, pulsing softly with sound.

Locations:

Scattered in dead-end corridors, hidden behind pipes or gates, or in rooms requiring environmental puzzle solving.

Some orbs are in areas that are safe early game, but become dangerous post-evolution.



The environmental puzzles range from easy to hard:

Easy: Valve Turning- turn valves in the correct order
Medium: The crystal will be locked behind a door that require a pass code 
Hard: Using an object to get another object in order to open a path to get a crystal

Additional Puzzle Ideas
🔶 (Medium) Light Beam Reflection Puzzle
Concept: Use reflective crystal shards or mirrors to direct a beam of light to a receptor that opens a gate.

Gameplay:

Player can rotate or move mirror-like crystals.

Only when light hits the right receptor does the crystal orb get revealed.

Some mirrors may be cracked (fakeouts), or hidden under slime/grime.

Twist:

While adjusting mirrors, creature briefly appears in the reflections.

Tension: if beam stays active too long, it heats up and makes noise.

🔷 (Medium/Hard) Circuit Relay Puzzle
Concept: Player must rewire a fuse box by connecting colored wires or toggling switches in the correct configuration.

Gameplay:

Sparks and flickering lights as feedback.

Cables can be followed visually through pipes or walls.

Connect red to red, green to green, etc. with misleading extras.

Twist:

Turning on power may also open other areas... including where the creature is waiting.

🟣 (Easy-Medium) Pipe Pressure Puzzle
Concept: The orb is behind a waterlogged gate. You must divert or reduce pressure in pipes to drain the chamber.

Gameplay:

Pipes hiss and rumble — you rotate wheels or close vents.

Gauges fill or drop depending on your inputs.

When water fully drains, orb is reachable.


🔺 (Hard) Sound Pattern Puzzle
Concept: Listen to a sequence of tones or drips, then replicate it on a sound panel or chime set.

Gameplay:

Player listens to a 4–5 note pattern.

Then taps on pipes or crystal shards that each make a sound.

Must repeat pattern to open the orb chamber.

Twist:

Wrong sound triggers an echo — alerts creature.

🟢 (Medium) Crystal Growth Puzzle
Concept: Feed energy or liquid to a crystal vine to grow it along a wall, eventually breaking through a weak grate.

Gameplay:

Redirect a glowing chemical or charge from a canister.

Crystal slowly grows (visible animation).

Once it reaches the grate, it shatters it.

Visuals:

Vines pulse with light when fed.

Orbs grow out of them — living crystals.

🔘 (Easy) Color Matching Lock
Concept: Colored valves or dials must be rotated so their crystals match a color pattern nearby (e.g., red-blue-green).

Gameplay:

Crystals light up when color is correct.

Feedback through glowing lights or hums.

Can be randomized per playthrough.

🧩 Bonus: Optional Lore Puzzle (for secrets or upgrades)
Collect logbooks, crystal samples, or broken keycards.

Arrange them in a lab console to reconstruct the origin of the creature.

Completing it gives you a temporary crystal light shield or reveals a secret orb.




2. Orb Collection
Player Interaction:

Walk up and press E (or interact key).

Brief glow + chime sound.

Orb disappears and a counter appears (Orbs: 3/8).

UI Feedback:

Orb counter in HUD.

Mini radar or vague sonar pinging if needed for accessibility (toggleable).



3. Progressive Danger Mechanic
Each orb collected escalates danger:

0–3 Orbs:

Crystalline creature moves in straight lines, only turns at corridor junctions.

Audible crystal crunching sounds when it's near.

You can predict and avoid it by watching line-of-sight patterns.


4 Orbs:

EVOLUTION TRIGGERED: Creature becomes humanoid (bipedal).

Now stalks player using semi-random patrols.

Can crouch, wait, or backtrack.

Emits low crystal hum; footsteps audible.


5–7 Orbs:

Creature gains increased speed and can chase when line-of-sight is triggered.

Will occasionally "teleport" or phase to nearby shadows (visual cue: shimmer).


8 Orbs:

Door is powered and now accessible.

Creature becomes enraged: ambient lights flicker, sewer shakes, loud screeching.

Final chase segment to the door.

Suggested development flow:
Phase	Focus
1	Player controller + basic UI + interaction system
2	Basic graybox environment (ring corridors + hub + rooms)
3	Basic AI movement + early player flow testing
4	Add puzzles (valves, doors, object collection)
5	Refine AI (mutation behavior, stalking logic)
6	Art pass on environment (models, textures, sounds)
7	Polish player feedback (screen shake, SFX, music tension)
8	Full test → iterate → final release
